Why These Guidelines Exist
Every button, every copy string, every color, every payment flow, every feature decision on Matchr must be anchored to something. These guidelines are that anchor. Before any pixel is pushed, any line of code written, or any copy approved — it must pass through this framework.

Matchr serves adults who come to the platform in different emotional states, with different intentions, and at different points in their desire cycle. The platform must serve all of them without breaking the experience for any of them. That is a hard design problem. These guidelines exist so we solve it once and consistently — not case by case.

01 — The Psychological Foundation
Every decision on this platform starts here. Before anything else — ask: which user state does this serve? Does it break any other state?

Matchr users exist in one of three states at any given moment. The platform must serve all three simultaneously without creating friction between them.

1.3 The Core Psychological Loop
Matchr must engineer a habit loop that replaces passive consumption (Pornhub, free adult content) with active interaction. The loop has five stages:

	•	Trigger — The user feels desire. Instead of opening a browser, their brain reaches for Matchr. This requires consistent positive associations built over repeated sessions.
	•	Frictionless entry — The user is inside the relevant experience within 10 seconds of opening the app. No long flows, no mood-breaking steps.
	•	Escalation — The interaction intensifies naturally. The platform creates the conditions for this (privacy, tools, creator incentives) without forcing it.
	•	Peak and satisfaction — The user reaches the emotional or physical outcome they came for. This moment must feel good — not transactional, not hollow.
	•	Return pull — After the session, the user carries a memory of a real human interaction. That memory pulls them back. This is what Pornhub cannot replicate.

The goal of every design decision is to strengthen this loop. If a feature does not serve at least one stage of this loop, question whether it belongs on the platform at all.

2.2 Psychology-Backed UI Rules
	•	Every CTA must reduce friction, not introduce it. If a user in an arousal state has to make more than two decisions to reach an interaction, the flow is broken.
	•	Coin depletion must never kill the moment. If a user runs out of Gold mid-call, the refill flow must be a single tap that returns them to the interaction within 5 seconds.
	•	Discovery cards must create desire within 1.5 seconds. Preview video autoplay, name and age immediately visible, call/gift CTA accessible without scrolling.
	•	The gift send flow must feel celebratory, not commercial. Animation, visual feedback, and the creator's reaction matter as much as the transaction itself.
	•	Privacy signals must be visible throughout — not just in settings. Users sharing intimate content need to feel protected at the moment of sharing, not after.
	•	Status indicators (VIP tier, verified badge, online now) must be contextually placed — not buried. They drive both creator selection and spender aspiration.
	•	Loading states must maintain mood. Blank screens, spinners, and error states break the psychological state the platform is trying to build. Every loading state needs design attention.

3.2 Coin Continuity Rule
A user who runs out of Gold mid-session must never be left stranded. The top-up flow must be a single-tap interruption that returns them to the exact interaction state they were in. This is the highest-priority payment UX rule on the platform.

	•	Pre-warn at 20% Gold remaining — a soft banner, not a modal
	•	At 0 Gold — pause the billable action (call), show single-tap top-up
	•	Top-up package pre-selected to the most popular bundle
	•	On payment confirmation — resume call automatically, deduct nothing for the pause window
	•	Maximum 3 taps from warning to back-in-call

3.3 Privacy as Infrastructure
Privacy is not a feature — it is the foundation of trust that makes intimate interaction possible on this platform. Users who do not feel protected will not share intimate content. Without intimate content, Matchr has no product.

	•	Screenshot blocking (FLAG_SECURE on Android) must be active on all screens showing user-generated intimate content
	•	View-once content must be server-deleted immediately on confirmed view, not scheduled — the deletion is the trust signal
	•	Privacy status must be visible at the moment of sharing, not in settings
	•	Any privacy failure — detected leak, screenshot attempt — must be communicated to the content sender within seconds
	•	Watermarking must be transparent to the creator: they must know their content carries an identifier

3.4 Data Architecture Rules
	•	Financial data (coin ledger, transactions) must use atomic database transactions — partial states are never acceptable
	•	Creator earnings must be visible to the creator in real time — delayed earnings display undermines trust
	•	User activity data (online status, last active) must be accurate within 60 seconds
	•	Notification delivery must be sub-5 seconds for high-priority events (match, gift received, call request)

04 — Economic Guidelines
4.1 The Spending Psychology
Gold must never feel like money. The moment a user thinks 'this costs me $20', the emotional transaction is interrupted. Gold is status currency — it represents presence, attention, and access.

Economic Principle
Implementation Rule
Gold, not dollars
Display all costs in Gold. Never show dollar equivalents during interaction. Dollar amounts only appear at the purchase screen.
Bundle psychology
The most popular bundle gets a visual distinction (star, glow). The top bundle always offers the best value per Gold. Users should feel smart buying more.
Gifting as status, not payment
When a user sends a gift, the UI celebrates them — the gift animation, the creator's reaction, the Gold deduction confirmation. This moment must feel like applause, not checkout.
Top-up as seamless continuation
Coin depletion is a retention risk. The refill experience must be so frictionless that users do not experience it as interruption — they experience it as investment.
Whale protection
High-spending users (top 5%) must be identified and treated differently — concierge service, shadow VIP, exclusive access. Public leaderboards can trigger whale avoidance behavior.
Creator earnings transparency
Creators must see their earnings in real time. A creator who cannot see their balance growing has no incentive to perform. Real-time earnings are a behavioral driver.

5.2 The Atmosphere Rule
The most important moderation goal is not content policing — it is atmosphere preservation. A platform where users feel unsafe, harassed, or disrespected will lose both creators and spenders. The atmosphere IS the product.

	•	Any user who makes another user feel unsafe must be acted upon within 24 hours of report
	•	Creators must have easy, non-disruptive ways to block and report — without leaving the interaction they are in
	•	Spenders who exhibit predatory behavior toward creators (harassment, threats, non-payment exploitation) must be removed
	•	The moderation team must understand the platform's intent — the goal is not to sanitize adult interaction, it is to ensure all interaction is consensual and safe
