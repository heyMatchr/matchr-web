2.3 The Three First Impressions
Research principle: a brand is defined by the first three words that come into a user's mind when they encounter it. For Matchr, those words should be:

PRIVATE  ·  REAL  ·  MINE

Private: The user feels they are in a protected, discreet space. Not public. Not observed.
Real: There is a real human on the other side. This is not artificial, not scripted, not a content library.
Mine: The experience feels personal and chosen, not mass-produced. My creator. My community. My terms.

3.2 Color Rules
	•	Void Black is the only background color. No white backgrounds, no light mode at MVP.
	•	Matchr Violet is the only primary CTA color. No green buttons, no blue buttons, no red buttons for primary actions.
	•	Gold is reserved for status and earnings contexts only. Do not use gold for general UI accents.
	•	Signal Green is reserved for presence signals only — online, verified, active. Do not use for any other purpose.
	•	Never use more than three colors on a single screen. Background + Violet + one supporting color maximum.

4.2 Typography Rules
	•	Never use decorative or script fonts anywhere on the platform. They read as cheap in adult contexts.
	•	Body copy line height must be 1.5x at minimum. Intimate messages need breathing room.
	•	Status labels must be all caps with 2-4px letter spacing. They must be readable at a glance, not demanding of attention.
	•	Gold and coin amounts must use tabular figures so numbers align consistently across balances and histories.
	•	Never use font weight to carry meaning alone — always pair with color or positioning to reinforce the hierarchy.

5.1 The Split M — Primary Mark
The Matchr M is built from two strokes that meet at a single violet node. Two paths. One meeting point. The entire brand concept lives in one letterform. This is not decorative typography — it is a symbol that means something.

5.2 Logo Rules
	•	Never stretch, rotate, or alter the proportions of the mark.
	•	Minimum clear space: equal to the height of the capital M on all sides.
	•	Never place the logo on a background that reduces the contrast below 4.5:1.
	•	Never use a drop shadow on the logo.
	•	Never recreate the M in a standard font — it must always be the custom SVG stroke construction.
	•	Never add tagline or supporting text below the mark when used at small sizes (< 24px mark height).

08 — Brand Application Rules
8.1 What Success Looks Like
	•	A new user opens Matchr and within 3 seconds feels: this is different, this is premium, this is for me.
	•	A creator opens Matchr and within 3 seconds feels: this is a platform that respects what I do and wants me to earn.
	•	An investor sees the brand and says: a senior creative director put this together, not a developer with a CSS file.
	•	The brand is consistent across every surface — app, pitch deck, social media, email, legal documents.
	•	The brand evolves without losing its core: Void Black + Violet + Gold + the Split M are the immovable elements.

8.2 What Failure Looks Like
	•	Any screen that could be mistaken for Tinder, Bumble, or a dating app — brand failure.
	•	Any screen that looks like a pornographic website — brand failure.
	•	Inconsistent use of color: a green CTA button, a blue badge, a purple header and a gold headline on the same screen — brand failure.
	•	Copy that says 'Find your soulmate' or 'Get laid tonight' — brand failure on both ends.
	•	A logo recreation in a standard font because the SVG was not available — brand failure.
